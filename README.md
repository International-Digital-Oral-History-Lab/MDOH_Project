# MDOH — Multimodal Digital Oral History

MDOH (Multimodal Digital Oral History) is a research project developing **methodologies and technical workflows** for active engagement with the **oral, aural, and sonic affordances** of oral history collections—across both **retro-digitised** and **born-digital** materials.

Building on Hidden Histories’ foundation, MDOH treats oral history artifacts as **multifaceted resources** rather than text-only objects. We work across multiple representational modalities—such as **transcripts, audio, waveforms, spectrograms, and metadata**—and use these modalities as analytical categories of inquiry.

A central aim is to advance digital oral history beyond passive digitization and online dissemination by embracing the **“sound as data”** turn: exploring how data-driven methods can generate new insights from oral history as **sonic and multimodal** artifacts, both at the level of individual interviews and at scale.

MDOH is also committed to **reflexive digital practice**. While we use computational approaches, we remain attentive to oral history as a **subjective and intersubjective meaning-making process**, situated within specific cultural, temporal, and technological contexts.

## What this repository is for

This repository is the main public hub for publishing and maintaining MDOH-related:

- **Data** (e.g., annotations, derived features, metadata extracts, evaluation sets—subject to rights and ethics)
- **Methods & documentation** (research protocols, workflow documentation, methodological notes)
- **Code** (pipelines, utilities, experiments, notebooks, reproducible scripts)
- **Outputs** (figures, tables, sample exports, schema definitions)

------

## Current research focus (example use case)

We are currently exploring **laughter detection** in oral history interviews using AI tools **in combination with non-digital approaches**.

Laughter is a compelling example of sonic elements—such as **hesitations, vocal quirks, and pacing**—that are often omitted from manual transcripts but can be analytically significant.

## Workflow orientation

MDOH supports multimodal analysis across:

- **Transcript** (including time-alignment where available)
- **Audio** (primary sonic record)
- **Waveform & spectrogram** (signal-level representations)
- **Metadata** (catalogue context, descriptive fields, segmentation)
- **Derived annotations** (e.g., laughter events, paralinguistic markers)

Where relevant, project workflows connect these representations to enable inspection, interpretation, and downstream analysis (including visualisation and editorial workflows).
